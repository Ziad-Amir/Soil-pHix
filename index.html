Soil pHix

Develop a sustainable feedback system to optimize bean crops cultivation, by monitoring soil pH and CO2 levels produced by microorganisms
With solar power and advanced technology, we are revolutionizing agriculture practices

About Code

Our design requirments

pH level

The pH of the soil is critical factor in ensuring optimal plant growth, our automated sustaiable system is designed to continuosly monitor and adjust the pH levels to create the ideal growing environment

Microorganisms amount indicated by CO2 level

Our system also takes into account the presence of microorganisms in the soil, which play vital role in nutrient cycling and plant health, by measuring soil repiration, we can accurately monitor the activity of these bacteria

Solar power

To power our system, we rely on renewable energy sources, that use our enviromental footprint, and also ensures that our system can operate independently in remote agriculture locations

Our vision

Our sustainable and efficient system is ideally suited to be applied in new agricultural projects in Egypt, including the National service projects that have already been established in several locations with a total area of 34,000 feddans that distributed on, Abu Sultan, the Muhammad Naguib sector, and east Ismailia, as well as the Fayoum governorate.
By incorporating our system into these new projects, farmers in Egypt can benefit from the accuracy of our system and the benefits it provides in terms of soil health and crop productivity. Our system can be applied to a range of crops, including beans and wheat, which are staples of the Egyptian diet and important for export. With the potential to reduce soil degradation, water pollution, and reduced crop yields, our system can have a significant impact on the environment and the livelihoods of farmers and communities.
By utilizing our system, farmers in Egypt can take a proactive approach to managing their land and crops, reducing the risks of negative consequences and ensuring that their farming practices are sustainable and efficient. This will not only benefit the farmers themselves but also contribute to Egypt's food security and position the country as a leader in sustainable agriculture.
After successfully developing and testing our prototype, we are excited to implement our sustainable system on a larger scale in Egypt. With the accuracy of our system being a key feature, farmers can rely on it to make informed decisions about how to manage their crops and soil, reducing the risk of human error and ensuring that their farming practices are sustainable and efficient.
