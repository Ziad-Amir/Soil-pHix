About Our prototype

The capstone project aims to develop a sustainable system that monitors and optimizes the growth of microorganisms in soil for improved crop productivity, addressing the Grand Challenges of grade 11 by promoting sustainable agriculture practices and improving food security. The system will use pH and CO2 levels resulting from microorganism respiration, powered by solar energy, and will have a feedback system that detects altered pH levels and initiates substrate-induced respiration (SIR) to monitor microorganisms' presence. A Nano Arduino will connect pH and CO2 sensors to transmit feedback signals and secrete glucose into the soil to increase CO2 production. The feedback system will analyze pH levels and secrete appropriate amounts of sulfate or lime to neutralize altered levels. This system is expected to provide an efficient and sustainable method to monitor and improve soil microorganism populations, ultimately enhancing crop productivity and food security, while providing hands-on STEM experience for students.
